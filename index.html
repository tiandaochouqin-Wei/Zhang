Introduction

Wei Zhang is currently a postdoctoral researcher in radiology at the Department of Clinical Sciences, Karolinska Institute, Sweden. He previously served as an assistant researcher in the School of Computing, Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT), where he received his Ph.D., and also underwent joint training with the Royal Institute of Technology in Sweden. His research interests primarily include medical image processing, deep learning algorithms, robot vision, and remote sensing image processing. In recent years, his research has focused on low-dose CT image denoising, brain tumor segmentation, lung nodule detection, and PET/CT image fusion and quality assessment. His findings have been published in journals and conferences such as Knowledge-Based Systems, Expert Systems with Applications, IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing (TGRS), Pattern Recognition, International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer-Assisted Intervention(MICCAI), and International Conference on Bioinformatics and Biomedicine(BIBM). He continues to participate in research related to medical artificial intelligence, image analysis, and multimodal learning. In addition to scientific research, he serves as an editorial board member, youth editorial board member and guest editor for several journals, and provides peer review services for journals and conferences such as IEEE TNNLS, IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, CVPR, ICCV, and ICASSP.
